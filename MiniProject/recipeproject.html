Gulab Jamun

Ingrediants

  • Sugar
  • Khoya(mawa)
  • Ghee
  • Cardmom
  • Baking Powder
  • All purpose flour
  • Water
  • Saffron
  • Rose water

Recipe

  1. Combine sugar and water in a pan and bring to a boil to create the syrup. Remove from heat,
    add cardamom, and set aside to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, paneer, sooji, Nestlé MILKMAID, baking powder, and baking soda.
    Mix gently to form a soft dough, avoiding over-kneading.
  3. Divide the dough into 30-35 portions and gently shape them into round balls.
  4. Heat oil on low flame and fry the balls until they turn golden brown.
  5. Transfer the fried Gulab Jamuns into the cooled sugar syrup. Once all the Gulab Jamuns are added,
    bring the syrup to a boil again briefly, then remove from heat.
  6. Enjoy your homemade Gulab Jamun warm, garnished with your favourite toppings.

Kaju katli

Ingrediants

  • 3 tbsp full cream
  • 2¼ cups flour
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ cup condensed milk
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • ½ tsp ground cardamom
  • ½ cup butter, cold and cubed
  • 3 tbsp icing sugar
  • Sprinkles

Recipe

  1. In a small bowl combine Dairy Dairy and water, then set aside.
  2. To a large bowl add flour, ginger, cardamom and butter. Cut butter into flour until a crumbly mixture is formed.
  3. Add Dairy Dairy and condensed milk to the flour mixture and stir to incorporate before lightly kneading the dough for 1 minute.
  4. Let the dough rest for 10 minutes.
  5. After 10 minutes, pinch off approx 1½ tbsp pieces of dough and form the gulab jamun.
  6. Fry on medium heat for 5 to 6 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Allow gulab jamun to cool and prepare the barfi drizzle by combining water, Dairy Dairy, icing sugar and spices.
  8. Drizzle the barfi mixture over the cooled gulab jamun and decorate with sprinkles.

Rosgulla

Ingrediants

  • 1 litre milk
  • 1/4 cup curd
  • few icecubes
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • a generous pinch cardamom powder
  • 5 nos pistachios for garnish

Recipe

  1. Heat milk, bring it to boil. Then add curd and keep stirring. Allow it to curdle.
  2. Stir continuously till the whey water clears and the milk curdles completely. Now switch off the stove,
    add ice cubes and allow it to melt completely. You can clearly see the whey water now.
  3. Take a muslin cloth and pour the curdled milk, filtering the whey water completely.
    Wash it well in running cold water if you have used lemon juice to remove the lemon flavour.
  4. Squeeze the excess water and hang it aside for 30mins without disturbing it.
    After 30mins, the paneer would be crumbly in texture.
  5. Now knead it well for atleast 20mins. This is the most important step as kneading for more
    time ensures soft and spongy rasgullas, so don't lose patience. See how soft the dough has become after kneading for 20mins.
  6. Now pinch a small portion of the dough, form smooth balls and keep aside.
    I made around 20 balls, Set aside.
  7. Heat water in a wide bottomed vessel, then add sugar.
  8. Keep it in flame and keep stirring till sugar dissolves completely, Simmer for 5mins.
  9. When the sugar syrup boils and starts bubbling, add the balls slowly one by one.
    The balls will go to the corner, move them to the middle.
  10. Keep in medium flame and close with a lid. Cook covered for 10mins.
  11. After 10 mins, the balls would be closely doubled in size.
    Switch off and keep it closed aside for 15mins.
  12. Then cool down and add chopped pistachios and chill it until serving time.

Jalebi

Ingrediants

  • 1 cup Urad dal soaked
  • 2 tablespoon Rice flour
  • 2 teaspoon Rosewater
  • 2-3 drops Orange Food color optional
  • 2 cups Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • ¼ teaspoon Cardamom powder
  • 1 teaspoon Lime juice
  • Oil for frying

Recipe

  1. Soak Urad dal for 1-2 hours in water.
  2. Put the dal in a blender jar with rice flour. Add 2 tablespoons of ice-cold water.
  3. Blend till a smooth paste is formed. Add more water if needed. But add little at a time.
  4. When a smooth batter is formed, transfer the batter to a big bowl.
  5. Add food color and beat the batter to make it airy using a circular motion of your hands. Give it a good 5-7 minutes.
  6. Let it rest at room temperature for about 45 minutes.
  7. In the meantime prepare the sugar syrup by adding 2 cups of sugar to 1 cup of water.
  8. Bring it to a boil. If the sugar syrup is dirty, add a tablespoon of milk and bring it to a boil again.
    Now remove the dirt accumulated at the top with a ladle. Repeat until a clear syrup is obtained.
  9. Now add cardamom powder and rose water.
  10. Keep simmering till the syrup attains one string consistency. At this stage add lemon juice, mix and turn off the flame.
  11. Heat oil to fry the Imarti. Keep the flame on low to medium heat.
  12. Pour the batter into a piping bag and cut depending upon the thickness you want in your Imarti.
  13. Shape Imarti by first making two circles clockwise and then making small swirls over them in an anticlockwise fashion.
  14. Pipe 4-5 Imartis at a time. Similarly, make all imartis in small batches.
  15. Fry them well on both sides.
  16. Remove from oil and dip in the hot syrup. Let it soak syrup for 3-4 minutes.
  17. Drain excess syrup and place them on a plate.
  18. Repeat till the batter is finished.
  19. Serve hot with chilled Rabri or as it is.

Soan Papdi

Ingrediants

  • 1 1/4 cup Gram flour (besan)
  • 250 gm ghee
  • 1 1/2 cup Water
  • 1 teaspoon crushed lightly green cardamom
  • 1 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk

Recipe

  1. Sift the gram flour and maida together. Keep aside.
  2. Heat ghee in a heavy pan over medium flame.
  3. Add the sifted flour and roast until light golden. Remove and keep aside
  4. Combine the milk and water in a pan.
  5. Place it over low-medium flame and add the sugar. Stir well to mix evenly.
  6. Simmer until it reaches a two thread consistency.
  7. Pour this into the flour mixture and mix well until the mixture forms thread like flakes.
    This method involves mixing and pulling the mixture apart alternatively.
  8. Transfer this into a greased plate and pat it flat to a 1 inch thickness.
  9. Sprinkle cardamoms, almonds and pistachios on top. Gently press them and even the surface.
  10. Allow it to cool.
  11. Cut it into 1 inch squares. Cover with the polythene squares
  12. Store them in an airtight container. Serve as a dessert.

Peda

Ingrediants

  • 200 to 205 grams Khoya
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons milk
  • 3 to 4 pistachios

Recipe

  1. To a pan add 1 tablespoon ghee, 1/4 cup sugar, 1/2 cup milk,
    2 tablespoon condensed milk. This is done off the stove.
  2. Whisk well until sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Now add 1 cup milk powder to it.
  4. Whisk well without any lumps.
  5. Now switch on flame and keep the pan and start cooking.
  6. Keep stirring in low flame.
  7. Keep stirring and it will start to thicken.
  8. Keep mashing and cooking if it forms lumps.
  9. When it comes together like this add 1 tablespoon ghee.
  10. Cook until the mixture leaves the sides of the pan like this.
  11. .Roll between your fingers, if it easily forms a ball then it is perfect time to switch off.
  12. Switch off transfer to a plate, let it become warm then mix it well once.
  13. Grease palms, fingers with ghee first. Pinch a small portion and roll between your palms and flatten it slightly.
    Make a small dent in the middle.
  14. Repeat to finish. I got 13 pedas. Now add chopped nuts and saffron in each dent.

Misti doi

Ingrediants

  • 1 litre or 4 cups full fat Milk
  • ¾ cup Sugar
  • ¾ cup hung curd
  • A few saffron strands
  • Pistachios for garnish

Recipe

  1. Pour milk in a kadhai. Add a few saffron strands and heat for around 10 minutes till it is reduced to around 800ml.
    Once reduced, remove it from heat, cover and keep aside.
  2. While the milk is cooling, spread the sugar in a saucepan and heat on low-medium heat.
    Let it cook undisturbed till the sugar melts and starts to caramelise.
  3. Once you get a nice, amber caramel, add in 1 cup of the previously heated milk and cook while continuously stirring.
  4. Keep stirring till the entire caramel melts into the milk.
  5. Add this mixture to the remaining milk and heat for another 2 mins.
  6. Pour the entire milk mixture onto the hung curd and whisk well till everything is nicely combined.
  7. Take an earthen pot and coat a thin layer of yogurt in it.
  8. Add the milk mixture in it and cover it with a lid or foil.Keep it in a warm place for 4-6 hrs or overnight.
  9. Refrigerate it and enjoy chilled. You can add chopped pistachios or other nuts of your choice as a garnish.

Kalakand

Ingrediants

  • 3 litre milk
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ¼ tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp nuts, chopped

Recipe

  1. firstly, in a large vessel take 2-litre milk and get to a boil.
  2. once the milk comes to a boil add 2 tbsp vinegar and mix well.
  3. add vinegar until the milk curdles.
  4. drain off the milk and rinse with water. rinsing with water helps to remove sourness.
  5. now squeeze off the water and transfer to a large plate.
  6. mash the paneer to smooth texture. keep aside.
  7. in a large kadai, heat 1-litre milk.
  8. stir and get to a boil.
  9. continue to boil until the milk reduces to half.
  10. add ¾ cup sugar and mix until the sugar dissolves.
  11. further, add prepared paneer and mix well.
  12. continue to cook until the mixture starts to separate the pan.
  13. further, add ¼ tsp cardamom powder and mix well.
  14. now transfer the mixture to the tray and level up.
  15. top with chopped nuts and press gently.
  16. rest for 8 hours or until it sets completely.
  17. cut into pieces and is ready to serve.
  18. finally, enjoy kalakand recipe with chopped nuts for a week when refrigerated.
